# Privacy Policy — CS Notes

_Last updated: 6 July 2026_

CS Notes ("the app", "we", "us") is a personal note-taking app for Android. This
policy explains what data the app handles and how. By using the app you agree to
this policy.

## Who this policy is for

CS Notes has two modes:

- **Guest mode** — you use the app without an account. Your notes are stored
  **only on your device** and are never sent to us or any server.
- **Signed-in mode** — you sign in with your Google account to sync notes across
  devices. In this mode your notes are stored in Google Firebase (Firestore).

## What data we collect

**Guest mode:** none. No account, no personal data, no network transmission of
your notes.

**Signed-in mode:**

- **Account identity from Google Sign-In** — your Google account email address and
  the unique user ID Google provides. We use this only to authenticate you and
  associate your notes with your account. We do not receive your Google password.
- **Your notes** — the text content of the notes you create, stored in Google
  Firebase (Firestore) under your account so they can sync across your devices.

We do **not** collect location, contacts, device identifiers for advertising,
analytics profiles, or any data beyond what is listed above.

## How your data is stored and protected

- Notes and account data in signed-in mode are stored using **Google Firebase**.
- Data is **encrypted in transit** (TLS/HTTPS) between your device and Google's
  servers, and **encrypted at rest** on Google's servers.
- Access is restricted by **Firebase Security Rules** so that only your
  authenticated account can read or write your notes.

## How we use your data

Solely to provide the app's functionality: authenticating you and storing and
syncing your notes. We do **not** use your data for advertising or profiling.

## Sharing your data

We do **not** sell or share your data with third parties. The only processor
involved is **Google Firebase**, which stores your notes and handles
authentication on our behalf. Google's handling of this data is governed by
Google's own privacy policy: https://policies.google.com/privacy

The app also lets you export your notes to a file you choose, and share individual
notes to other apps — these actions are initiated by you and send data only where
you direct it.

## Data retention and deletion

- Your notes remain stored until you delete them.
- The app provides in-app deletion: you can delete individual notes, empty the
  trash, and use **Settings → Danger Zone** to delete all notes and/or delete your
  account and its associated data.
- Deleting your account removes your notes from Firebase.
- To request deletion outside the app, contact us at the email below.

## Children's privacy

CS Notes is not directed at children under 13 and we do not knowingly collect data
from them.
